## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Overview

Alumni Connect is a digital marketplace platform that enables verified university students and alumni to purchase and sell professional servicesâ€”including job postings, mentorship sessions, career events, and premium featuresâ€”within a secure, university-verified ecosystem.

The platform launches with **Ashesi University** as the pilot institution, leveraging its technologically literate community, strong alumni culture, and supportive administrative ecosystem. After validation at Ashesi, the platform will expand to additional universities across Ghana and West Africa.

### 1.2 Problem Identification

**For Students:**
- Limited access to structured, affordable career development services
- Difficulty finding verified mentors in their field of interest
- No centralized platform for university-specific opportunities

**For Alumni:**
- No convenient platform to monetize expertise while giving back
- Fragmented communication channels (WhatsApp, LinkedIn, email)
- Lack of structured mentorship frameworks

**For Universities:**
- Inability to track graduate career outcomes post-graduation
- Limited visibility into alumni engagement levels
- No data-driven insights for career services improvement

### 1.3 Proposed Solution

Alumni Connect addresses these gaps by providing:

1. **A Trusted Marketplace** - Verified users can buy/sell professional services
2. **Direct Monetization** - Alumni earn income from mentorship and services
3. **Career Tracking Analytics** - Universities monitor graduate success metrics
4. **Secure Payments** - Integrated Paystack processing with mobile money support

## 2. Problem Statement

### 2.1 The Student Challenge

University students in Ghana face significant barriers to career development:

1. **Limited Mentorship Access**
   - Only 15% of students report having access to formal mentorship programs
   - Existing programs are unstructured and difficult to navigate
   - No platform connects students with alumni in their specific field

2. **Career Opportunity Gap**
   - Job postings scattered across multiple platforms
   - No centralized source for university-specific opportunities
   - Difficulty identifying which alumni can provide industry connections

3. **Financial Barriers**
   - Professional career coaching costs GHS 500-2,000 per session
   - Students cannot afford traditional career development services
   - No affordable, peer-based alternatives exist

### 2.2 The Alumni Challenge

Alumni who wish to give back face their own obstacles:

1. **Time Constraints**
   - Busy professionals cannot commit to formal mentorship programs
   - No flexible scheduling tools for occasional mentoring
   - Difficult to manage multiple mentee relationships

2. **No Monetization Path**
   - Alumni cannot earn income from sharing expertise
   - No incentive structure for sustained engagement
   - Volunteering model leads to mentor burnout

3. **Verification Issues**
   - LinkedIn profiles can be fabricated
   - No way to confirm university affiliation
   - Students cannot trust unverified "mentors"

### 2.3 The University Challenge

Institutions struggle with alumni relations and career tracking:

1. **Graduate Outcome Tracking**
   - Manual surveys have <20% response rates
   - No real-time data on graduate employment
   - Accreditation bodies require employment statistics

2. **Alumni Engagement**
   - Difficult to measure alumni participation
   - No visibility into mentorship activities
   - Cannot identify high-potential alumni donors

3. **Career Services ROI**
   - Cannot correlate mentorship with job placement
   - No data to justify career services budget
   - Unable to identify underserved student populations

1. **Individual Mentorship Sales**
   - Alumni list sessions at GHS 100-200
   - Platform takes 18% commission
   - Student pays, alumni delivers session

2. **Corporate Sponsorship Packages**
   - Companies sponsor mentorship for students
   - Example: "Tech Ghana" sponsors 50 sessions for CS students
   - Company pays bulk rate: GHS 6,000 (50 Ã— GHS 120)
   - Platform handles matching, scheduling, tracking
   - Revenue: GHS 6,000 per corporate package

3. **University-Sponsored Programs**
   - Ashesi Career Services sponsors 100 sessions/semester
   - Bulk rate: GHS 10,000/semester
   - Platform manages all logistics
   - Annual revenue: GHS 20,000 (~$1,250)

4. **Premium Mentorship Bundles**
   - "Career Accelerator" - 4 sessions over 2 months: GHS 500
   - "Interview Prep Bundle" - 3 mock interviews + resume: GHS 400
   - "Leadership Track" - 6 sessions with C-level alumni: GHS 800

### 6.3 Database Schema

```sql
-- Core E-Commerce Tables

CREATE TABLE services (
  service_id INT PRIMARY KEY AUTO_INCREMENT,
  service_name VARCHAR(200) NOT NULL,
  service_type ENUM('job_posting','mentorship','event','premium_feature'),
  description TEXT,
  price DECIMAL(10,2) NOT NULL,
  duration INT,
  stock_quantity INT DEFAULT 1,
  provider_id INT,
  is_active TINYINT DEFAULT 1,
  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP
);

CREATE TABLE cart (
  cart_id INT PRIMARY KEY AUTO_INCREMENT,
  user_id INT NOT NULL,
  service_id INT NOT NULL,
  quantity INT DEFAULT 1,
  selected_date DATE,
  special_requests TEXT,
  added_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  FOREIGN KEY (user_id) REFERENCES users(user_id),
  FOREIGN KEY (service_id) REFERENCES services(service_id)
);

CREATE TABLE orders (
  order_id INT PRIMARY KEY AUTO_INCREMENT,
  user_id INT NOT NULL,
  order_number VARCHAR(50) UNIQUE,
  order_status ENUM('pending','processing','completed','cancelled'),
  payment_status ENUM('pending','success','failed','cancelled'),
  subtotal DECIMAL(10,2),
  tax_amount DECIMAL(10,2),
  total_amount DECIMAL(10,2),
  payment_reference VARCHAR(100),
  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP
);

CREATE TABLE payments (
  payment_id INT PRIMARY KEY AUTO_INCREMENT,
  order_id INT NOT NULL,
  payment_reference VARCHAR(100),
  payment_method VARCHAR(50),
  amount DECIMAL(10,2),
  currency VARCHAR(10) DEFAULT 'GHS',
  payment_status ENUM('pending','success','failed','cancelled'),
  paystack_response TEXT,
  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  FOREIGN KEY (order_id) REFERENCES orders(order_id)
);
```
